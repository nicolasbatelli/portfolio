3D printing opened up a new creative outlet for me alongside software development. Here's what I've learned so far.

## Start simple

Before designing complex parts, print calibration cubes and basic models. They teach you about bed adhesion, layer height, and material behavior.

## Design for printing

- Avoid steep overhangs without support
- Consider print orientation early
- Add fillets to reduce stress concentrations
- Design parts in multiples of your nozzle width when precision matters

## Tools I use

- **Fusion 360** for parametric CAD
- **Blender** for organic shapes
- **PrusaSlicer** / **Cura** for slicing
